What's new and what's next

The Age of Gas: Efficiency and Economics

Jens Svenson CEO, GE Sweden | March 16, 2014

We're excited to announce GE Ventures' most recent investment in RainDance Technologies, an innovative genomics tools company making molecular testing of complex diseases more standardized and readily available. RainDance is making heroic strides in the research and development of products that improve patient care and save lives.

Preventative care is the future of healthcare, and early detection will play a key role. However, current testing for complex diseases is both expensive and invasive to patients. That's why GE is always looking for partners that are developing cutting edge technologies that will offer patients cost-effective, accurate diagnoses and faster, more effective treatments.

GE Ventures partnership with RainDance will not only accelerate research for investigative laboratory procedures but also help drive commercial expansion. In other words, they make their research a reality for patients and we couldn't be more excited to be a part of it.

RainDance is currently developing new 'liquid biopsy' methods which may one day help doctors identify cancer-causing mutations. The procedure uses needles to collect tiny droplets containing DNA, a much less invasive and cost efficient approach then surgical biopsies.

Marketing as a Forethought

John Richards Senior Vice President and Chief Financial Officer, GE | March 05, 2014

Traditionally, the first steps of a startup are marked by a strong focus on the product or offering. However, a strong product is nothing without a strong reputation. Yet, one of the elements most overlooked by entrepreneurs is actually one of the most important, one that needs to be considered from the very beginning: the power of the Brand.

A strong brand explains why you exist in the world, but it doesn't stop there. Communicating your brand effectively will get you noticed and help people identify with you. The stronger your brand recognition, the more people will be interested in you, partnering with you, investing in you and coming to work with you. So being accessible, human and approachable should be a goal for startups from day one.

Easier said than done, right? Especially in today's market, where there are endless mediums and avenues to reach the public. From traditional marketing tactics, to more recent social and digital tools, there is no wrong answer. But finding what works best for your product and market is the challenge. I think about this more than anything else, and I'm excited to share my learnings with you.

Mocana - Securing the Internet One Device at a Time

Elizabeth Canters SVP and Treasurer, GE & GE Capital | March 03, 2014

When it comes to the Industrial Internet and the Internet of Things (IoT), cyber security is vital to protect the critical infrastructure that connects machines and devices. And as data from these assets continues to proliferate, so do the opportunities to exploit that data.

So what's necessary to keep us ahead of the growing tide of security threats? Mocana knows. Which is why we're investing in them – the device and app security leader. Mocana's goal is to simplify the deployment of secure mobile solutions for the Industrial Internet and IoT, and speed enterprise mobility adoption by automating app security. Mocana will use the investment to innovate and scale, but they will also have access to GE experts and our global network of customers and partners to bring their new ideas to market faster.

Their technology is already in use on millions of devices around the world – in markets such as medical devices, consumer electronics and industrial automation. Even GE uses Mocana for a number of things. It's a part – but only one part – of everything we need to do to maximize the security of GE products, which are diverse, ranging from MRI machines to smart meters to rail signals.
