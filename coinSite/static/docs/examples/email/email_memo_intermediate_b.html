Generate clean recycled water for industrial use with ZeeWeed* hollow-fiber membranes

The North Saskatchewan River Basin in Edmonton, Alberta, Canada, is one of the many places that needs to preserve the quantity and quality of its available water. That's why the city of Edmonton approached a local refinery, Petra Canada, with a collaborative municipal/industrial proposal to reuse the water from the Gold Bar Wastewater Treatment Plant. Petra Canada was able to use the wastewater plan's recycled water for some of its processes and reduce its freshwater withdraws by over 7 billion liters a year and could depend on the infrastructure of the Gold Bar Wastewater Plant in order to supply its water needs. By using the ZeeWeed 500 series, the Gold Bar Wastewater Treatment Plant provides the following for the local refinery:

  • Consistently excellent water quality
  • Less equipment fouling at the refinery
  • A more efficiently run facility

In the end, the Gold Bar Wastewater Treatment Plant's use of the ZeeWeed 500 series enabled Petta Canada Refinery to use recycled water for some of its processes, aiding both the environment and its own bottomline.
